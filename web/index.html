Wave Reflection Analysis

Three-probe separation of incident and reflected waves · Goda & Suzuki (1976), Kobayashi, Cox & Wurjanto (1990)

Methodology

This tool implements the three-wave-probe reflection analysis used by Lamsal, Haus & Rhode-Barbarigos (2026), An experimental study on wave transmission over submerged SEAHIVE® breakwaters, Coastal Engineering Journal (doi:10.1080/21664250.2026.2661171).

Following the methodology of Goda & Suzuki (1976) and Kobayashi, Cox & Wurjanto (1990), incident and reflected wave heights are separated from the records of three co-linear capacitance wave probes using linear wave theory. Two probe arrays are deployed — one seaward and one shoreward of the structure. For each array the complex wave amplitude at the wave frequency is obtained from a Hann-windowed discrete Fourier transform of each gauge record (the DC offset is removed first). The wave number k follows from the linear dispersion relation ω² = gk tanh(kd), and the incident and reflected amplitudes ai, ar are recovered by complex least squares over the three gauges: Aj = aie−ikxj + are+ikxj.

The method is valid when the probe spacing Δl satisfies 0.05 ≤ Δl/L ≤ 0.45, where L is the local wavelength; outside this range a gauge pair becomes ill-conditioned and the tool automatically falls back to the best-conditioned pair. In the SEAHIVE experiments, water levels were sampled at 100 Hz on either side of the structure under regular (monochromatic) waves. Wave height is twice the recovered amplitude, and the coefficients are defined as Kt = Ht/Hi and Kr = Hr/Hi.

Regular vs. irregular waves. For regular (monochromatic) waves the separation is performed at the single dominant frequency, detected from the signal spectrum, and the wave height is twice the recovered amplitude. For irregular (random) waves the separation is performed at every frequency bin; incident and reflected spectra are integrated to spectral significant wave heights Hm0 = 4√m0 (a faithful port of the WaveLabX three_probe_array method). Each input file holds six time-series columns: channels 1–3 form probe array 1 (seaward → Hi1, Hr1), channels 4–6 form probe array 2 (shoreward → Hi2, Hr2). Water depth is set below and may be overridden per file.

Settings

Array 1 — ch 1–3 (seaward)

X12 = spacing between gauges 1 and 2; X23 = spacing between gauges 2 and 3, in metres. Gauge 1 is the reference (position 0).

Array 2 — ch 4–6 (shoreward)

X45 = spacing between gauges 4 and 5; X56 = spacing between gauges 5 and 6, in metres. Gauge 4 is the reference (position 0).

Each array is defined by two gauge spacings; gauge positions are taken as 0, X12, X12+X23. Water depth and gauge layout apply to every file; wave frequency is detected per file and is editable in the table.
